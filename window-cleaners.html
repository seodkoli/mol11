How to Clean Exterior Windows Safely and Effectively

With the right tools and techniques, you can clean your windows like a pro in no time. First things first, gather all the necessary supplies. You will need a bucket, squeegee, microfiber cloth or sponge, window cleaning solution (or a mixture of water and vinegar), and some old newspapers for drying. Start by removing any dirt or dust from the window frame and sill using a dry cloth. Next, prepare your cleaning solution by mixing water and vinegar in equal parts in your bucket.

Wipe down the entire surface of the window, paying special attention to any stubborn spots or streaks. Once you have cleaned the window with the soapy solution, it's time to use the squeegee to remove any excess water. Starting at the top corner of the window, pull the squeegee across in a straight line, wiping off any water that collects on the blade with a clean cloth after each pass. Continue this process until you have completed the entire window.

Finally, use an old newspaper to buff away any remaining moisture and leave your windows sparkling clean and streak-free. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for windows to dry after professional cleaning?

The drying time depends on factors such as weather conditions, temperature, and humidity levels, but windows typically dry within a few hours after professional cleaning.

How can I prepare for a professional window cleaning appointment?

Before the appointment, remove any obstacles such as furniture or decorations blocking access to windows and ensure that windows are accessible and not obstructed by landscaping.

Do I need to remove screens before professional window cleaning?

It's generally recommended to remove window screens before professional cleaning to ensure that both the glass and screens are cleaned thoroughly.
