What is the proper technique for cleaning windows with delicate or decorative features?

How Often Should You Clean Your Windows? A Maintenance Guide

Cleaning windows with delicate or decorative features requires a gentle touch and careful attention to detail. These types of windows can be easily damaged if not cleaned properly, so it's important to use the right technique to keep them looking their best. The first step in cleaning windows with delicate or decorative features is to gather the necessary supplies. You will need a mild glass cleaner, a soft cloth or sponge, and a gentle brush for any intricate detailing. It's also helpful to have a bucket of warm water and some clean towels on hand.

This will help prevent dirt from being spread around while you clean the glass. Next, dampen your cloth or sponge with the glass cleaner and gently wipe down the window pane in small circular motions. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive materials, as these can damage delicate features. For windows with intricate designs or detailing, use a soft brush to gently clean around these areas.

Rinse the brush frequently in warm water to ensure that you are not spreading dirt around. Once you have finished cleaning the window pane, rinse it off with clean water and dry it thoroughly with a soft towel. Make sure to remove any streaks or smudges for a flawless finish.
